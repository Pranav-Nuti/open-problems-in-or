Problem #2

Tight Bounds for Online Bipartite Matching

Author Vahab Mirrokni, Rad Niazadeh
Difficulty Medium
Chance of Being Open Medium

Preliminary Notation

Consider a bipartite graph \( G = (L \cup R, E) \) where:

  • \( L = \{u_1, u_2, \ldots, u_n\} \) are "offline" vertices (known in advance)
  • \( R \) are "online" vertices that arrive one at a time
  • When vertex \( v \in R \) arrives, we see its incident edges and must irrevocably match \(v\) to at most one neighbor in \(L\)

Let \( \text{ALG}(\sigma) \) denote the size of the matching produced by algorithm ALG on arrival order \( \sigma \), and \( \text{OPT}(\sigma) \) the maximum matching size. The competitive ratio is: \[ \inf_{\sigma} \frac{\mathbb{E}[\text{ALG}(\sigma)]}{\text{OPT}(\sigma)} \] where the expectation is over any randomness in ALG.

Problem Statement

Open Problem: For the adversarial online bipartite matching problem (vertices on one side arrive in adversarial order), the classic Ranking algorithm achieves a competitive ratio of \( 1 - 1/e \approx 0.632 \). It is known that no online algorithm can achieve better than \( 1 - 1/e \) (Karp, Vazirani, Vazirani; 1990).

However, under random-order arrival (ROA), where the arrival order is uniformly random, the best known lower bound is approximately \( 0.696 \) (Manshadi et al., 2012), while the best known upper bound is \( 0.823 \) (Huang et al., 2022).

What is the exact competitive ratio achievable under random-order arrival? Is there an algorithm that achieves \( 0.7 \) or higher? Can we close the gap between \( 0.696 \) and \( 0.823 \)?
