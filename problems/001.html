Problem #1

Polynomial-Time Algorithm for Stochastic Queueing Networks

Author J. Michael Harrison

Preliminary Notation

Consider an open queueing network with \(K\) service stations. Let:

  • \( \lambda_i \) denote the external arrival rate to station \(i\)
  • \( \mu_i \) denote the service rate at station \(i\)
  • \( P = (p_{ij}) \) be the routing matrix where \(p_{ij}\) is the probability a job completing at station \(i\) goes next to station \(j\)
  • \( \rho_i = \lambda_i / \mu_i \) be the traffic intensity at station \(i\)

The network is stable if there exists a unique solution \( \lambda = (\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_K) \) to the traffic equations: \[ \lambda_i = \alpha_i + \sum_{j=1}^{K} \lambda_j p_{ji} \] where \( \alpha_i \) is the exogenous arrival rate. We require \( \rho_i < 1 \) for all \(i\) for stability.

Problem Statement

Open Problem: Does there exist a polynomial-time algorithm that, given an open Jackson network or a more general stochastic queueing network (e.g., with general service time distributions), decides whether the network is positive recurrent (stable)?

For Jackson networks, stability is characterized by the condition \( \rho_i < 1 \) for all stations, which can be checked in polynomial time. However, for networks with:

  • General (non-exponential) service time distributions
  • Multiple job classes
  • Blocking or finite buffers
  • State-dependent routing

the stability region and its computational complexity remain poorly understood. In particular: Is the stability problem for generalized stochastic queueing networks in P, NP-hard, or undecidable?
