Problem #4

Approximation Algorithms for the Traveling Salesman Problem with Time Windows

Author David Williamson

Preliminary Notation

Let \( G = (V, E) \) be a complete graph with \( |V| = n \) vertices, metric edge costs \( c_{ij} \geq 0 \), and for each vertex \( i \) a time window \( [a_i, b_i] \). A feasible tour visits each vertex exactly once, starting and ending at a depot (vertex 1), and arrives at each vertex \( i \) within its time window. Waiting is allowed.

Formally, a tour is a permutation \( \pi \) of \( \{1, \ldots, n\} \) with \( \pi(1) = 1 \). The arrival time at vertex \( \pi(k) \) is: \[ t_k = \max\left\{ t_{k-1} + c_{\pi(k-1),\pi(k)}, \; a_{\pi(k)} \right\} \] with \( t_1 = 0 \). Feasibility requires \( t_k \leq b_{\pi(k)} \) for all \(k\). The objective is to minimize total travel time \( \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} c_{\pi(k),\pi(k+1)} + c_{\pi(n),1} \).

Problem Statement

Open Problem: The Traveling Salesman Problem with Time Windows (TSPTW) is strongly NP-hard. For the metric TSP (without time windows), the best approximation ratio is \( 1.5 \) (Christofides-Serdyukov). For TSPTW:

  • No constant-factor approximation is known in the general metric case
  • On the line (1D), a PTAS exists (Tsitsiklis, 1992)
  • For the "nice" case where time windows are "reasonable" relative to travel times, some heuristics perform well empirically

Does there exist a constant-factor approximation algorithm for metric TSPTW? What is the best approximation ratio achievable (assuming P ≠ NP)? Is there a \( O(\log n) \)-approximation?
